StopWatch

A stopwatch is a handheld timepiece designed to measure the duration of an event with high precision. It typically features buttons for starting, stopping, and resetting the timing, often with a lap/split function to record intermediate times without interrupting the main count. Modern stopwatches are commonly digital, displaying time in hours, minutes, seconds, and hundredths of a second, though analog versions with sweeping hands still exist. Essential in sports, scientific experiments, and various professional fields, the stopwatch is a fundamental tool for accurate timekeeping and performance analysis. Read more
